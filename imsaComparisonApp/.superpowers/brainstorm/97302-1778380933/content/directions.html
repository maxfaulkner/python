Direction A
The Race Debrief
"Stop looking at charts. Start reading the race."

The app becomes a race analyst. You pick a session and it generates a narrative — automatically identifying the key moments, the pace leaders, the hidden battles. Charts serve the story, not the other way around. It's like a post-race engineer debrief, except the data does the talking.

Direction B
The Driver Fingerprint
"Every driver has a signature. This is where you find it."

Forget single-session comparisons. The app builds a full performance identity for every driver — a radar of their strengths across dimensions: qualifying vs. race pace, wet vs. dry, early-stint vs. late-stint tire management, home circuits vs. away. Then you compare fingerprints. "Who is this driver, and who races like them?"

Direction C
The Circuit Book
"Every track has a personality. Some drivers get it. Most don't."

Flip the mental model entirely. Start with a circuit, not a driver. The app becomes a living encyclopedia of every track — who dominates here and why, how conditions affect lap times, the all-time lap record progression, which manufacturers suit the circuit's character. Watkins Glen rewards different skills than Daytona. This is where you prove it.
